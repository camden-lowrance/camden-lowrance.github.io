Current Foundation

Architecting the Core

At Beck's, I built the modern farm data pipeline from scratch, replacing fragile, monolithic server architectures with resilient, cloud-native services.

This system acts as the absolute source of truth for the farm record. The software operates precisely at the chaotic intersection of noisy field hardware and structured data models. Without it, the core product goes entirely dark.

Operating at this scale means relentlessly managing both high-throughput synchronous APIs and deeply compute-intensive asynchronous workers. Engineering this level of unflinching reliability has been the necessary prerequisite for introducing next-generation, agent-driven logic to the pipeline.

Future Trajectory

Charting AI-First Workflows

The transition from manual scripting to autonomous software requires deep architectural shifts. Here is where my focus currently drives:

Agentic Orchestration

Autonomous Reasoners

I am designing systems where intelligent models don't just summarize data, but act over it. This involves engineering agents that can autonomously navigate operational constraints, logically synthesize field telemetry, and orchestrate underlying services without human intervention.

Resilient Infrastructure

Bulletproof Foundations

You cannot build smart systems on brittle data. I continue to drive the core computational paths from raw, noisy inbound streams to clean, persistent spatial models. Uptime and absolute correctness are what allow agentic systems to succeed.

Distributed Architecture

Decoupled Scaling

I maintain the delicate balance between synchronous interaction layers and heavy async execution. True scale—and the computational overhead required by modern AI tools—requires specialized workers that operate asynchronously to keep the user experience seamless.
